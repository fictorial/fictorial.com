Overview ▲

In Lexatron, a match consists of two players, two letter racks, a letter bag, and the board which consists of spaces on which letters may be placed to form words. The first player has green letters while the second player has orange letters.

Each player's letter rack consists of up to 7 letters chosen randomly from the letter bag.

Gameplay ▲

Each player takes a turn until the match ends. A player may do any of the following to take a turn:

  • take one or more letters from their letter rack and place them on the board to form one or more words
  • pass their turn
  • resign the match
  • swap one or more letters from their letter rack with replacement letters selected at random from the letter bag

On a player's first placement of letters on the board, one of the letters must be placed on the player's start space. The first player's start space is the top-left space, and is green with an arrow on it. The second player's start space is the bottom-left space, and is orange with an arrow on it.

Thereafter, one of the words formed in a turn must include a letter from a word formed earlier by the same player.

Words extend horizontally and vertically just like in other word games. In Lexatron however, horizontally means "diagonally up to the right" while vertically means "diagonally down and to the right". That means words formed on the board must be read in either the left to right direction or up to down direction.

Words can of course overlap your other words already on the board.

Words formed do not take into account the letters of words already formed by the opponent. While it is possible to form a word with a letter adjacent to an opponent's letters, it is not possible to form a word that spans a word of the opponent. Objectives ▲

A player's primary objective in Lexatron is to reach their end space before their opponent reaches their end space.

A player's secondary objective is to score as many points as possible. Should the players be blocked from reaching their respective end spaces, a match may still be won by having the most points when the letter bag is fully depleted.

Swapping ▲

A player may swap or exchange one or more letters from their letter rack. After selecting which letters to swap, the player's turn ends. There must be at least 7 letters in the letter bag for swapping to be allowed.

Blank Letters ▲

There are a number of blank letters in the letter bag at the start of a match. When placed on the board (or moved around on the board in the same turn), the player is prompted to pick a substitute letter to represent the blank. The substitute letter has a point value of 0 but of course may be used to form words.

Scoring ▲

Each letter has an assigned point value. Each word formed is the sum of the point values that comprise the word.

The scoring of letters and words may be increased by placing letters atop modifier spaces.

  • 2L doubles the point value of the letter
  • 3L triples the point value of the letter
  • 2W doubles the point value of the word formed
  • 3W triples the value of the word formed
  • ! is a one-time bonus of 25 points

Modifiers only take effect during the turn in which they are encountered. For instance, words formed by extending a word that contains a letter atop a 3W does not triple the extended word's point value.

A player earns a 50 bonus points for using all 7 letters in their letter rack in a single turn.

Game-over Conditions ▲

A match ends when any of the following occur:

  • a player reaches their end space before their opponent reaches their own end space
  • a player resigns the match
  • both players pass their turns twice consecutively
  • no letters remain to replenish player racks and both player racks are empty

In the latter two cases, the winner is the player with more points.

Dictionary ▲

Lexatron uses a slightly modified ENABLE1 (Enhanced North American Benchmark Lexicon) dictionary as its source of supported words. Some derogatory words have been removed to keep everybody happy; we hope you understand. Please visit the following URL to view Lexatron's dictionary:

http://fictorial.com/lexatron/dictionary.txt

Match Duration ▲

Online matches may take a while to finish with turns sometimes taking days. This is normal. Start more matches if you'd like! You can play up to 25 simultaneous matches. Don't worry... if your opponent fails to play a turn after some time, you will be declared the winner.

Letter Distribution ▲

The distribution of letters in the letter bag at the start of each match is as follows.

A: 18, B: 4, C: 4, D: 10, E: 12, F: 4, G: 6, H: 8, I: 16, J: 2, K: 2, L: 8, M: 4, N: 10, O: 16, P: 4, Q: 2, R: 12, S: 10, T: 14, U: 8, V: 4, W: 4, X: 2, Y: 4, Z: 2, blank: 4

Letter Point Values ▲

The point values for each letter are as follows.

A: 1, B: 4, C: 5, D: 2, E: 1, F: 4, G: 3, H: 3, I: 1, J: 10, K: 5, L: 2, M: 5, N: 2, O: 1, P: 4, Q: 10, R: 1, S: 1, T: 1, U: 2, V: 5, W: 4, X: 8, Y: 3, Z: 10, blank: 0
